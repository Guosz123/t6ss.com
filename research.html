Research

Core directions and ongoing themes in the T6SS Laboratory

Research Areas

1) Plant–microbe interactions in the root zone

We investigate how beneficial microbes colonize plant tissues and how plants shape microbiome assembly through root exudates and immune signaling, with implications for growth promotion and disease resistance.

Rhizosphere Endosphere Root exudates

2) Microbial suppression of soilborne diseases

We study microbial antagonism and community-level disease suppression against important soilborne pathogens, aiming to develop robust, environmentally friendly biocontrol strategies.

Biocontrol Soilborne pathogens Disease suppression

3) Synthetic microbial communities (SynComs)

We design and validate synthetic microbial consortia to improve plant performance and stabilize beneficial functions under greenhouse and field conditions.

SynCom Community engineering Field validation

4) Multi-omics for mechanism and translation

We integrate amplicon sequencing, metagenomics, transcriptomics, and metabolomics to link microbial composition with function, and to identify actionable traits for microbial product development.

Amplicon Metagenomics Transcriptomics Metabolomics

Selected Keywords

Plant–microbe interactions; rhizosphere microbiome; endophytes; soilborne diseases; biocontrol; synthetic communities; multi-omics; sustainable agriculture.
